Welcome to Our Global CO2 Emissions Analysis

This interactive narrative visualization explores various factors that influence CO2 emissions across different countries. Navigate through our three scenes to discover intriguing insights about how education, land use, and population dynamics relate to carbon emissions.

Scene 1: Education Enrollment vs CO2 Emissions

Explore the relationship between education enrollment rates (both primary and tertiary) and CO2 emissions. This visualization also factors in each country's GDP, allowing you to see how economic development intersects with education and environmental impact. Interact with the chart to filter countries by GDP and discover surprising correlations.

Scene 2: Land Area, Forested Area, and CO2 Emissions

Investigate how a country's land area and forest coverage relate to its CO2 emissions. This scene provides insights into the role of natural carbon sinks in offsetting emissions. Use the interactive filter to explore how different levels of forest coverage impact emissions across countries of varying sizes.

Scene 3: Population Dynamics and CO2 Emissions

Examine the complex relationship between population size, urban density, and CO2 emissions. This bubble chart visualization offers a unique perspective on how urbanization and population distribution affect a country's carbon footprint. Interact with the bubbles to uncover detailed information about each country's demographic and emissions profile.

Click on each scene to dive deeper into these global patterns and relationships. Our interactive visualizations allow you to explore the data, uncovering insights that challenge common assumptions about the drivers of CO2 emissions.

Dataset Information

The visualizations in this project are based on the "Countries of the World 2023" dataset. This comprehensive dataset provides a wide range of information about countries worldwide, including demographic, economic, and environmental data.
